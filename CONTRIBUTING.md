# Contributing to The Unification Architecture Manifesto

This repository hosts a **philosophical and architectural thesis**, not software. Contributions are welcome, but they must align with the document's purpose, tone, and strategic boundaries.

## 🎯 What we accept
- **Clarifications & precision:** Wording improvements that enhance technical accuracy, regulatory alignment, or architectural clarity without changing the thesis.
- **Translations:** Professional-grade localization for regulated markets (EU, LatAm, APAC). Translations must preserve the original tone, structure, and technical terminology (e.g., `system boundary` / `límite entre sistemas`).
- **Regulatory mappings:** Addenda or footnotes that map principles to specific frameworks (AI Act, FDA SaMD, DO-178C, BCBS 239, etc.) without turning the manifesto into a compliance checklist.
- **Typographical & structural fixes:** Markdown formatting, broken links, consistency corrections.

## 🚫 What we do not accept
- Feature requests, roadmap suggestions, or technical runtime discussions (those belong in the main `unifylang` repository).
- Marketing language, buzzwords, or vendor comparisons.
- Changes that dilute the core thesis: *unification without migration, audit as cryptographic evidence, governance at the boundary*.
- Rewrites that shift the tone from architectural/philosophical to promotional or sales-oriented.

## 📝 How to contribute
1. Fork this repository.
2. Create a branch named `clarification/...`, `translation/...`, or `fix/...`.
3. Submit a Pull Request with a clear rationale explaining how the change improves precision, accessibility, or regulatory alignment.
4. Maintain the original structure, section headings, and four principles.
5. All contributions are reviewed for tone consistency, technical accuracy, and alignment with the unification thesis.

## 🌍 Translation guidelines
- Preserve the distinction between **integration** and **unification**.
- Use `límite entre sistemas` (ES), `system boundary` (EN), or equivalent architecture-native terms. Avoid `costura`, `integración`, or `middleware` when referring to the unification layer.
- Keep the tone sober, technical, and enterprise-ready. This document is read by CTOs, CROs, regulators, and chief architects.
